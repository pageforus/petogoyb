Our Backstory

Phetogo ya Botlhe was born in the heart of Atteridgeville in 2020 during a time of desperation and need. When food insecurity hit our township hardest during the pandemic, a few caring hands decided that no one should go to bed hungry. One of the founders, Mam Dineo Lebelo, started cooking in a traditional potjie over open flames using her own groceries. Word spread quickly, and neighbours, friends, and strangers started donating ingredients, firewood, and their time.

From humble beginnings on the dusty streets of Atteridgeville, the project grew into a vital lifeline for many. Today, we feed over 200 families every week — with hope, dignity, and love in every serving. We believe food is more than nourishment — it’s a symbol of care and humanity.
